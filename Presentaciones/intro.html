Coordenada $\chi$

If U and K are uniform, they can be brought outside the integral, and:

$z(x)=z(x_b)+(\frac{U}{KA_0^m})^{1/n}\int_{x_b}^x\frac{dx}{A(x)^{m/n}}$

To create transformed river profiles with units of length on both axes, it is convenient to introduce a reference drainage area, $A_0$

$z(x)=z(x_b)+(\frac{U}{KA_0^m})^{1/n} \chi$

where the longitudinal coordinate is equal to:

$\chi=\int_{x_b}^x(\frac{A_0}{A(x)})^\theta dx$

and $A_0$ is an arbitrary scaling area that gives $\chi$ units of length. The slope of the χ‐z plot is $k_{sn}$ if $A_0$ is set to unity
